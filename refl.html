Reflective Practice

Reflective Practice

Reflective practice is an active process that involves observing your own teaching experiences in order to analysis them closely. It entails examining your daily activities to identify strengths and weaknesses, allowing you to become aware of challenges and discover effective solutions to address them.

Coverage

  • School of Practice: Nana Akosuah Akyeamaah Junior High School
  • Subject Taught: Computing
  • Topic: Social Media
  • Date: 24th January, 2025
  • Class: 7
  • Duration: One Hour
  • Reference: Current Computing Textbook

Relevant Previous Knowledge (R.P.K.)

Students were familiar with the uses of social media.

Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • Define Social media
  • Give three examples of social media with two examples each
  • Mention two types of social media
  • List three uses of social media

TLMs

Pictures/videos of social media platforms and current computing textbook

Activities

Introduction: Through questions and answers, teacher guides learners to brainstorm how social media works through the internet.

  1. Teacher guides learners to discuss the meaning of Social media.
  2. Teacher guides learners to brainstorm how social media works through the internet.
  3. Through questions and answers, teacher writes the ceiling points on the board for students to copy.
  4. Teacher guides learners to brainstorm the characteristics of social media.

Core Points

Social media: Online platforms or tools that allow users to create, share, and interact with content, information, or other users.

Three examples of social media:

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Instagram

Types of Social media:

  • Social networking site (e.g., Facebook, LinkedIn)
  • Microblogging platforms (e.g., Twitter, Tumblr)

Three uses of social media:

  • Connecting with friends and family
  • Sharing information and experiences
  • Networking and professional development

Strengths of the Lesson

  • Lesson objective clearly stated
  • Orderly presentation of the lesson
  • Classroom management and class control was good
  • Excellent use of Teaching and Learning materials

Weaknesses of the Lesson

  • Student involvement could have been improved

Innovative Solution

I would use more examples and also employ diverse teaching and learning approaches such as group discussions and presentations helps improve student involvement lesson delivery.

Remarks

The lesson was successful, as most learners performed very well in the assessment given to them.
