Teaching Philosophy

Introduction

Teaching philosophy is a reflective articulation of one’s beliefs regarding teaching and learning. It can also be described as a framework through which educators examine their values related to teaching (Harrison, 1990). Essentially, a teaching philosophy outlines a teacher's convictions and details how these beliefs will be put into practice in the classroom to promote effective teaching and learning outcomes.

Statement of My Teaching Philosophy

As an aspiring teacher, I firmly believe that “students learn best when they are actively engaged in the teaching and learning process.” This conviction shapes my teaching philosophy, which emphasizes a learner-centered approach that prioritizes the unique needs and learning styles of each student. I encourage students to actively participate in their education, as this engagement significantly enhances their ability to comprehend the material. Utilizing a variety of illustrations, examples, and appropriate teaching resources, I aim to create a dynamic classroom environment where learning is far from abstract or passive.

There is a wise Chinese saying that goes, “I see and I remember, I hear and I forget, I do and I understand.” This adage underscores the importance of a hands-on approach, demonstrating that students are more likely to internalize and understand concepts when they are actively involved in the learning process.

Implementation

Every student's contribution is vital to the teaching and learning process, playing a crucial role in lesson delivery. To foster this, I encouraged students to engage in extensive reading to uncover knowledge independently. I implemented a question-and-answer approach, ensuring that questions were evenly distributed and that I gave equal attention to each student’s input and ideas. I also urged students to respect and value the perspectives of their peers, discouraging any mockery of answers that might be deemed incorrect. I emphasized that no one, not even myself, possesses all knowledge. Keeping this in mind, every student felt empowered to share their thoughts during discussions, which greatly enhanced the classroom environment.

Validity

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that my approach is the most effective and appropriate way to facilitate learning. I am committed to ensuring that every student, regardless of race, religion, socioeconomic status, or any other factor, has an equal opportunity to learn. I view all students as equals, as they come to school to receive an education. I believe that students learn best through active collaboration with their peers, which fosters teamwork. My classroom management plan is designed to instill self-discipline in students, empowering them to take responsibility for their own behavior. To validate and implement my teaching philosophy, I employ techniques such as discussions and the equitable distribution of questions, ensuring that every student is actively engaged in the learning process.
